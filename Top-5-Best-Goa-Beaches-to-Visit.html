Top 5 Best Goa Beaches to Visit.

15.k views

Goa is a state on the southwestern coast of India within the Konkan region, geographically separated from the Deccan highlands by the Western Ghats. It is bound by the Indian states of Maharashtra to the north, and Karnataka to the east and south, with the Arabian Sea in the west.
North Goa is visited by large numbers of international and domestic tourists each year because of its white-sand beaches, active nightlife, places of worship, and World Heritage-listed architecture. It also has rich flora and fauna because it lies very close to the North Western Ghats rainforests, one of the rare biodiversity hotspots of the world.
It’s no secret that North Goa is a favorite beach destination in India which offers top notch NightLife in North Goa. It has a laid-back lifestyle, hippie vibe, glorious scenic beaches, natural tranquility, beach sports, and awesome parties. It’s a great destination year-round, but visiting Goa during summer is quite a special experience.

Historical Fact of the Capital City of Goa:
Panaji is the state's capital, while Vasco da Gama is its largest city. The historic city of Margão in Goa still exhibits the cultural influence of the Portuguese, who first voyaged to the subcontinent in the early 16th century as merchants, and conquered it soon thereafter, whereupon Goa became an overseas territory of the Portuguese Empire, part of what was then known as Portuguese India, and remained as such for about 456 years until it was annexed by India in 1961.

Some of the Top 5 Beach Tourist Destinations in North Goa:

There are various things to do in Pahalgam. Below are some of the famous listed attractions and adventure activities in Pahalgam, that every visitor and tourist can experience for a fun-filled visit.

  1. Baga Beach:

    Water sports, upscale dining options, pubs, and clubs can all be found at Baga Beach, one of Goa's finest beaches. Baga Beach is located in North Goa and is surrounded by the beaches of Anjuna and Calangute. At Baga Beach, water sports are the main attraction. The most popular water sports in Baga Beach are wakeboarding, jet skiing, windsurfing, kite surfing, and parasailing. From the beach, one can also go on island excursions and dolphin-watching activities.
    Along with having some of Goa's best nightlife, Baga also offers some of the best pubs and clubs, especially along Tito's Lane. One unique attraction at Baga Beach is an indoor snow park that has a play area, sledding area, and ice bar. For shopping fans, the best place to explore is the Tibetan Market on Baga Road.

  2. Morjim Beach:

    Morjim Beach, situated in North Goa, is renowned for its serene shores and tranquil ambiance. Often referred to as "Little Russia," it has garnered significant popularity among Russian travelers. Notably, billboards and menus are readily available in the Russian language, catering to the preferences of the many Russian tourists who frequent the area. Moreover, Morjim Beach serves as a vital hatching ground for the endangered Olive Ridley Turtles, further adding to its ecological significance.

  3. Candolim Beach:

    Candolim Beach, one of the widest stretches of coastline in North Goa, has captured the admiration of tourists with its peaceful environment setting, diverse adventure sports, and recreational opportunities. Serving as the gateway to other renowned beaches in Goa, Candolim Beach spans approximately 30 kilometers from Fort Aguada to Baga Beach. While it maintains a comparatively peaceful ambiance, it remains a haven for water sports enthusiasts, offering activities such as parasailing and water skiing amidst its serene surroundings.

  4. Calangute Beach:

    Calangute is the beach to which everyone heads for the moment they land in Goa. So it's known as the most popular beach and it is overcrowded both in peak and off seasons. This huge seven kilometer sweep of sand located 15 kms from Panaji, is called the ‘Queen of Beaches’. All the travel agencies and tour operators offering adventure sports in North Goa have a base here from where bookings are done for most of all the other beaches.

  5. Anjuna Beach:

    Amongst the most popular beaches in Goa, Anjuna Beach has a rich past of hippie culture. However, this beach that stretches almost 2 km is also popular amongst families and water sports lovers. At night, Anjuna Beach becomes one of the best hubs in Goa for nightlife. Anjuna is also known for its Wednesday Flea Market.
    Anjuna beach Goa is an ideal choice for nature buffs, who love to enjoy full moon parties during the New Year and Christmas.


Things to do in North Goa:

North Goa is one of the best destinations for adventure sports activities. The main attraction of Goa is water activities. North Goa offers Scuba Diving, Dudhsagar Falls Trek, Snorkelling, Kayaking in Sal Backwaters, Aguada Fort Visit, Dolphin Spotting, Cruise ride at Mandovi River, Banana Boat Ride and many more water activities.

TRIPGYANI SPECIAL TIPS

  • Goa is home to one of the world’s most famous religious places and tourists from across the world come here to observe its elegance. Also, famous churches and temples are a few of the most sacred destinations to be explored here. However, proper etiquette and due respect should be showcased while visiting these religious places.

  • It's important to keep your essential documents safe. While traveling to Goa, all the important documents like driver’s license, passport, and visa should be kept in a hotel locker room.

  • Avoid carrying too much cash while you are going to Goa for your vacation. Popular places are often the targets of pickpockets and thefts. Carrying a substantial amount of cash without adequate precautions may increase the risk of robbery or financial loss. To avoid this misfortune, you can use plastic money which is more convenient.

  • Goa, well-known for its stunning scenery, is a favored destination for photography enthusiasts. Yet, it's crucial to be mindful of certain protocols. Always be on the lookout for signage indicating restrictions such as "No Pictures" or "Photography Restricted" before snapping away. Furthermore, many museums in Goa prohibit photography altogether. Respecting these regulations ensures a harmonious experience for all visitors and preserves the integrity of these cultural institutions.

  • Before ordering, be sure to check if you're vegetarian or prefer non-alcoholic options. If you have a sensitive stomach and don't enjoy spicy food, it's best to avoid street food. In Goa, meat-based dishes go beyond just fish, prawns, and chicken, so it's wise to confirm the contents of your order beforehand to avoid any regrets.

  • When enjoying the nightlife, it's important to exercise caution and stay away from drugs. Consumption of drugs is banned in India and is considered a non-bailable offense throughout the country.
